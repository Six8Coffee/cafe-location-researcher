Investigation question
What does publicly available data show about the $175k asking price against the Newtown café market — and what is the information this listing has not provided that would materially change any assessment?
What the sources show
Bsale currently lists 5–7 café and food businesses for sale in Newtown and the inner west at $95k–$240k. At $480k stated turnover, a $175k asking price implies a goodwill multiple of approximately 0.36× annual revenue. The typical range for a café with a solid lease and provable turnover is 0.5–1.5×. A below-range multiple signals a motivated seller, a thin net margin, a lease issue, or some combination. CommercialRealEstate.com.au shows current commercial leases on King St at $38,000–$68,000 per annum for café-sized spaces.
Where sources conflict
"Profitable" and a below-typical goodwill multiple are in tension. A genuinely profitable café with $480k turnover and 4 years of lease security in Newtown would typically attract a higher multiple — unless the net margin is thin when owner wages are added back, the lease has an unfavourable review mechanism approaching, or the exit reason is something the broker isn't leading with.
Open questions
What is the current rent — and what is the review mechanism for years 2–4? A market review clause in year 2 on King St could substantially change the effective rent. Is there a demolition clause?
What this investigation cannot tell you
  • "Profitable" means nothing without a P&L with owner wages, depreciation, and super added back — a café doing $480k gross can net $20k or $80k depending on cost structure
  • Why this business is for sale — the most important question cannot be answered from any public source
  • Actual comparable sale prices — Bsale shows asking prices only; completed transaction prices are not publicly disclosed in Australia
The most important unresolved question
Request the full P&L for at least two years and the complete lease document before progressing. Without both, the asking price cannot be evaluated against anything that matters.

Use Olivia yourself

Three steps. Works in any Claude project.

1

Enable web search in your Claude project

Settings → Project Settings → enable Web Search. Olivia uses live data sources and cannot investigate without it.

2

Download and upload the folder

Get the files from GitHub and upload them into a Claude project. Claude uses them as operating context for every conversation.

3

Tell Olivia what you're investigating

"I'm looking at a suburb." "There's a listing on Bsale." "I'm about to sign a lease." She asks a few questions, then searches.
